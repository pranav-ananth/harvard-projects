Overall assessment
This is a strong, well-structured proposal with clear technical ambition. The multimodal approach is well-motivated and the pipeline architecture is thoughtfully designed. Below are specific observations across strengths and areas for improvement.
StrengthResearch motivation
The problem framing is compelling. The argument that marginal accuracy improvements translate to lives saved is clear, and the emphasis on underutilized data streams (wearables, unstructured notes) is novel and well-justified.
StrengthPipeline architecture
The heterogeneous graph schema (Figure 2) is sophisticated and clinically grounded. Modeling patients, diagnoses, medications, and signals as typed nodes with directed edges enables rich relational learning that tabular models cannot capture.
StrengthEvaluation strategy
Choosing MCC as the primary metric for an imbalanced dataset (32% positive class) is methodologically sound and aligns with the Chicco & Jurman baseline. Including SHAP values for explainability is clinically important and submission-ready.
SuggestionTime feature handling
The "time" feature (follow-up period) is the strongest predictor at 36.1% importance — but it may encode information unavailable at prediction time. Clarify whether this is a predictor or a survival analysis endpoint. Consider Cox PH or DeepSurv models.
SuggestionData acquisition plan
The proposal lists EPIC EHR, doctor notes, and wearable data as sources but the acquisition path is vague ("access will be sought"). For a research proposal, a concrete IRB/data sharing agreement plan with realistic timelines would strengthen credibility significantly.
SuggestionBaseline scope
The UCI dataset has only 299 patients — far too small to meaningfully train GNNs or Graph Transformers. The proposal should explicitly address this: the UCI baseline trains on tabular data only, and GNN models require the additional EHR/multimodal data acquisition first.
EnhancementSurvival analysis framing
The title mentions "survival analysis" but the proposal focuses on binary classification. Consider formalizing the survival component with Kaplan-Meier curves and log-rank tests as an additional evaluation layer — this would better match the title claim.
EnhancementMissing data strategy
Real-world EHR data will have substantial missing values. The proposal mentions imputation for tabular data but the multimodal case (missing echo, missing wearable) needs a dedicated strategy — e.g. modality dropout during training to build robustness.
RiskScope creep
Integrating 7 data modalities (clinical, EHR, notes, wearables, ECG, echo, CXR) is ambitious to the point of being a multi-year project. For a student research project, consider scoping to 2-3 modalities with a clear plan to extend — and frame the others as future work.
RiskReproducibility concerns
EPIC EHR, de-identified echo studies, and wearable data are very difficult to share publicly. This creates a reproducibility gap if the goal is open-sourcing. Consider pre-registering the study design and clearly distinguishing which parts of the pipeline are reproducible with public data only.
